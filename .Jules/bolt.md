## 2024-05-24 - Idiomatic Zipped Iterators Outperform Manual Loop Unrolling
**Learning:** In Rust vector operations (like cosine similarity calculations), manual loop unrolling and chunking via indexing (e.g., `a[i]`) can actually *inhibit* LLVM's auto-vectorizer due to complex control flow and potential bounds checking. Using idiomatic slice iterators like `a.iter().zip(b.iter())` leverages `TrustedRandomAccess` and `ExactSizeIterator`. This allows the Rust compiler to completely elide bounds checks and emit highly optimized, auto-vectorized SIMD instructions that are usually faster and much more readable than manual chunking.
**Action:** When optimizing array/vector mathematics in Rust, prefer idiomatic chained iterators (like `zip`) over manual loop unrolling and chunking. Let LLVM handle the SIMD vectorization automatically.
